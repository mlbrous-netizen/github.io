🧳 Ultimate 15-Day Packing Recommendations

Late May and early June in Europe means unpredictable weather. London will be breezy and potentially rainy, Paris can swing from hot and humid to chilly, and the Swiss Alps will require warm layers. Here is what you absolutely must pack for a smooth 15-day group trip.

🔌 Tech & Gadgets

  • Universal Power Adapters: You need adapters that cover the UK (Type G), France (Type E/C), and Switzerland (Type J).
  • High-Capacity Power Bank: Critical for 15-hour days using GPS and snapping photos at Disney and CERN.
  • Portable White Noise Machine: Or a dedicated Bluetooth speaker. When 10 people are sharing large flats in London and Paris, having reliable white noise to block out creaky floors and early risers is a lifesaver.
  • Noise-Canceling Headphones: Perfect for zoning out on the long Eurostar or TGV train rides while blasting the Epcot Harmonious soundtrack!
  • Multi-Port USB Charger: To charge phones, power banks, and watches off a single European wall plug.

🎢 The Hobby Kit

  • Disney Pin Lanyards & Books: Have these packed in a day bag for the Disneyland Paris leg. Remember to export that pin collection spreadsheet to your phone for offline viewing at the Pueblo Trading Post!
  • The RSVLTS Collection: Joe and Mike must ensure all 15 assigned KUNUFLEX™ shirts are washed and packed.
  • Custom Luggage Tags: Strap the multi-material 3D printed tags securely to the top handle of every group member's bag.
  • Lightweight Park Daypack: A small, comfortable bag for carrying water, ponchos, and souvenirs through the Disney parks and Versailles.

🧥 Clothing Strategy (Layering)

  • Broken-In Walking Shoes: Do not bring new shoes. You will be walking 15,000 to 25,000 steps a day. Bring two comfortable pairs to alternate.
  • Lightweight Rain Jacket: Essential for London and Paris. Choose something packable that fits in a daypack.
  • Warm Mid-Layer (Fleece/Sweater): Mandatory for the Mount Pilatus ascent in Switzerland, where it will still be very chilly.
  • Breathable Base Layers: Paris in early June can get unexpectedly hot, and many historical sites lack heavy air conditioning.

📂 Organization & Essentials

  • Packing Cubes: The absolute best way to manage a suitcase when unpacking and repacking across four different destinations.
  • Passports: Ensure they are valid for at least 6 months after your June 2027 return date!
  • Physical Credit Cards: While Apple/Google Pay are ubiquitous in Europe, always carry physical cards (preferably ones with no foreign transaction fees like the Chase Sapphire or Capital One Venture).
  • Travel Medical Kit: Blister pads, ibuprofen, and any daily prescriptions.
