  • Breathable Base Layers: Paris in early June can get unexpectedly hot, and many historical sites lack heavy air conditioning.

📍 Ultimate Disneyland Paris Pin Trading Guide
📱 The Lineberty App is Mandatory If you want to purchase any new Limited Edition (LE) pins (like the "Tales of Magic Castle" series or Bastille Day exclusives), you cannot just walk up to a register. You must secure a digital queuing ticket through the Lineberty App to purchase them at the Pueblo Trading Post.
The "WDW to Paris" Trade Economy

Disneyland Paris cast member lanyards are surprisingly full of standard Walt Disney World pins (you will frequently see Animal Kingdom or Monorail pins!). Because of this, local Parisian traders are not looking for basic US rack pins or standard Hidden Mickeys.

What to Bring from the US: If you want to make high-value trades with local French collectors, you need to bring WDW Exclusives. Pack EPCOT Festival exclusives (Food & Wine, Festival of the Arts), Mickey's Not-So-Scary Halloween Party pins, Disney Vacation Club (DVC) pins, and Walt Disney World Resort hotel pins. These are highly sought after because European collectors cannot get them without flying to Florida!
Paris Exclusives to Hunt For

Having your offline pin collection spreadsheet pulled up will make tracking these new European subsets incredibly satisfying.

• Attraction Exclusives: Look for pins featuring Phantom Manor (specifically the Western ghost town elements unique to Paris) and Space Mountain 2 (the Jules Verne steampunk design).
• Parisian Themes: The "Eiffel Tower Collection" (characters wrapped around the tower) and Mickey & Minnie dressed as Parisian locals (like Chef Mickey).
• Ratatouille: Since the movie is set in France and has a massive dedicated plaza in the Walt Disney Studios park, Remy pins are a major local staple.
Top Locations & Etiquette
• The Trading Hub: The Pueblo Trading Post in Frontierland is the absolute epicenter of Paris pin trading. It is primarily open on weekends, and local collectors gather at the tables outside to trade.
• The Rules: You can trade a maximum of 2 pins per Cast Member per day. Pins must be metal, feature the ©Disney mark, and have their corresponding fastener.
• Etiquette: Park guests in Paris do not wear lanyards as commonly as they do in the US. When interacting with Cast Members, never touch their lanyard or trading board without explicitly asking permission first.
