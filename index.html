📅 Recommended Booking Timeline

To ensure you secure the best prices and availability for a group of 10, follow this chronological booking strategy.

10-12 Months Out
Flights & Lodging:
• Flights: Major airlines generally release international flights roughly 330 days (11 months) in advance. Start tracking prices immediately for the multi-city routes (US to LHR, ZRH to US).
• Lodging & Disney Packages: Book the shared flats in London and Paris, the boutique hotel for the Austin duo, and the Disneyland Paris Marvel Hotel packages as soon as your dates are locked.
Immediately After Disney Booking
Disney Dining (Group of 8):
• Hotel Guests: Because you are staying at a Disney Hotel, you can book your table-service dining reservations (like Bistrot Chez Rémy and Captain Jack's) upon receiving your booking confirmation. Book these right away, as they are highly competitive!
• Non-Hotel Guests: For reference, guests not staying on property can only book up to 2 months (60 days) in advance.
6-8 Months Out
International & High-Speed Trains:
• Eurostar (London to Paris/Disney): Tickets typically go on sale 6 to 8 months in advance. Book these as soon as they drop for the lowest fares.
• TGV & TGV Lyria (France/Swiss): High-speed trains connecting Paris, the Loire Valley, and Geneva usually open for booking 3 to 6 months in advance.
• Swiss Domestic (SBB): Internal Swiss trains can open anywhere from 2 to 10 months in advance.
3-4 Months Out
Tours & Theater:
• Hamilton (London): Book your West End theater tickets for the entire group.
• Major Museums: Secure skip-the-line group tickets for the Louvre and any guided tours for Versailles or the Loire Valley châteaux.
1-2 Months Out
Final Details:
• Non-Disney Dining: Make reservations for the highly sought-after restaurants in London (Dishoom) and Paris (Le Coupe-Chou).
• CERN & Excursions: Finalize tickets for the CERN tour in Geneva and the Mount Pilatus cogwheel ascent in Lucerne.
📌 Appendix & Master Tips
🏛️ The "Start Day" Rule (Avoiding Museum Closures) Because many major European historical sites have strict weekly closures, Day 1 of this trip must fall on a Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, or Saturday.
  • The Louvre (Day 8): Closed on Tuesdays.
  • Palace of Versailles (Day 9 Option): Closed on Mondays.
  • Windsor Castle (Day 2 Option): Closed on Tuesdays and Wednesdays.
If you start the trip on a Sunday, Monday, or Tuesday, you will hit a closed door at one of these major locations.
🔌 The Power Adapter Gauntlet You are visiting three distinct regions that use entirely different electrical outlets. Everyone in the group will need a universal adapter or specific plugs for:
  • UK (London): Type G (Three rectangular pins).
  • France (Disney/Paris): Type E/C (Two round pins).
  • Switzerland: Type J (Three round pins in a triangle). Note: Standard Euro-plugs (Type C) will fit into a Swiss socket, but larger grounded plugs will not!
📍 Essential Transit Apps Have the group download these apps before leaving the US to make navigating the cities seamless:
  • Citymapper: The absolute best app for navigating London and Paris public transit (much better than Google/Apple Maps for subways).
  • SNCF Connect: Essential for managing your digital TGV train tickets in France.
  • SBB Mobile: The official app for Swiss travel; perfect for checking live train times and buying point-to-point tickets.
